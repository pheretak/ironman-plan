Plan adjustments — data-driven
All changes based on Apple Watch + Wahoo data from Days 1–27.

1. HR ceiling on easy runs

Runs averaged 157–162 bpm in Week 1. Ceiling set at 150 bpm easy, 148 bpm long run. Walk the moment you hit it. Your Zone 2 pace is 11'00"–11'30"/mi — it will feel slow. That's correct.

2. Mandatory walk warm-up

Every run starts with 8–10 min brisk walk. HR must be at 120–125 before running. This alone drops avg run HR by 5–8 bpm. Mile 1 pace: 12'00"–12'30" on fatigued days, never faster than 10'30" on fresh days.

3. Wahoo Zone 1: watch power

HR alone doesn't capture bike intensity. TSS 98 from a "Zone 1" session (VI 1.22) proves it. Watch power display in real time. NP ceiling 110W. VI target under 1.08. No surges above 140W on easy days.

4. Long run by HR not distance

Sunday long runs capped at 148 bpm average. If that means 3 miles instead of 5 — correct call. Distance follows aerobic base, not ambition. Mile 1 minimum 12'30" regardless of energy.

5. No double sessions

Saturday Apr 18 swim after run = stroke count doubled to 40–42, mechanics completely broken. Single sessions only. If you swim and run on the same day, one of them gets moved to another day.

6. Cadence alert active

Apple Watch Ultra set to buzz below 145 spm. Cadence improved from 123 to 150 spm in 11 days. Now holding 143–150 spm even under fatigue. Target for race day: 152–158 spm.

7. Recovery week structure

When 3+ consecutive sessions show HR 5+ bpm above baseline — take 2 consecutive full rest days, not 1. One rest day (Wed) followed by training (Thu) is insufficient when the fatigue is systemic.

8. Return to 25yd pool

16–17yd pool sessions produce wall-artifact stroke counts of 35–48 vs true 20–28. All pace and stroke data from short-course pools is non-comparable. Standard 25yd pool is the only valid baseline.

What stays the same: swim 3x/week, Wednesday rest + strength, evening/morning schedule, overall phase structure. Your swim is ahead of schedule — 1,546yd in 40 min with negative split on Day 27.
